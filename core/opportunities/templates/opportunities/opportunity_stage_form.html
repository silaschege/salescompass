About Pipeline Stages

Pipeline stages represent the steps in your sales process. Each opportunity moves through these stages as it progresses toward closure.

Key Fields:

  • Pipeline Type Reference: Associates this stage with a specific pipeline type (e.g., Sales, Support).
  • Name: A descriptive name for the stage (e.g., Prospecting, Qualification, Negotiation).
  • Order: Determines the sequence of stages in the pipeline.
  • Description: Details about what happens in this stage.
  • Probability: The likelihood of closing an opportunity at this stage (0-100%).
  • Won/Lost Stage: Mark if this stage represents a closed-won or closed-lost status.

Best Practices:

  • Keep stage names consistent across your organization.
  • Set realistic probabilities based on historical data.
  • Limit the number of stages to avoid complexity (typically 5-8 stages).
  • Regularly review and optimize your pipeline stages.
